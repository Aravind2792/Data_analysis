Introduction¶

This report is specific to the analysis of TMDB movie dataset. There are few questions that can be raised to help streamline the analysis on this dataset.

  1. How is budget associated with revenues?
  2. What are the characteristics of a production company leading higher revenues ?
  3. Does Higher popularity increases vote counts, thereby increasing the revenue of the film?
  4. Does Seasonal releases impact the revenue generation of a movie?
  5. What factors are associated with the failure of a film?
  6. What genres are most accepted?
  7. Which film was the most successful and which performed the worst in the 21st century? (2000-2015).

Observations¶

  • The data has about 10866 rows and 21 columns, out of which 5 columns has null values namely 'production_companies','keywords','homepage','tagline' and 'cast'.

  • There is one duplicated row and can be removed from the date.

  • The Revenue and budget column combined has about 7011 rows of zero values and can be considered as missing values because having any one of those column as zero wont give an insightful analysis and zero values for these columns are un-realistic.

  • Columns such as id,imdb_id,homepage,tagline and overview are not needed for the analysis and hence can be removed

Inferences¶

  1. The columns 'id','imdb_id','homepage','tagline','overview','keywords' were removed as it wasn't needed for this analysis.

  2. Filtered out the budget and revenue rows having zero values which was un-realistic.

  3. Removed the Null values rows.

  4. Changed the 'release_date' column to date format to use for suitable analysis.

  5. Normalized the 'revenue_adj' and 'budget_adj' columns to a common scale of million to make more uniform analysis.

  6. The 'Budget_adj' and 'revenue_adj' are rounded off to the closest upper limit as accuracy upto decimal values would not be needed in this insight. </p>

Conclusions¶

Summarizing my exploration on the research questions as listed below. I have done the exploration on this dataset to the best of my knowledge, however there can be several other factors or viewpoints that can still be explored which i may have missed. Hence this is just an self analysed exploration from my side.¶

    I have considered the following for my analysis:
  1. Revenue as the dependent variable, column name- 'revenue_adj'.
  2. Popularity, Vote average, Released month, Genres, Production companies as the independent variables.

1. Is Higher budget associated with higher revenues?¶

  1. The average revenue of 121.65 has exceeded the average budget of 40.08 along the years, showing that there may have been no deficits.

  2. However the from year 1985 the ratio between average revenue to average Budget has significantly decreased to 3.46 when compared to the ratio years before 1985 to 3.46
  3. Apart from the couple of outliers, higher budget movies have generally resulted in higher revenue, with the average budget of 115.2 million USD and average revenue of 341.8 million USD .
  4. From the distribution plots it can be said that both the parameters are right skewed , with Revenue understandably having a larger spread.

2. What are the characteristics of a production company leading to higher revenue ?¶

  1. The Top 5 highest revenue generating production company are the likes of Twentieth Century Fox, Warner Bros,Universal pictures,Walt Disney, Paramount pictures .
  2. All these high revenue production companies have been associated with a cast heavy plots like Kate Winslet, Leonardo DiCaprio, Sam Worthington, Chris Evans etc, among which most of the films belonged to Science fiction/Fantasy and thriller with ideal run time 120-140 minutes .(refer below plot)
  3. This shows that the audience might prefer watching more of the above mentioned genres with a complete story probably involving previous story plots like the one from Marvel studios

3. Does Higher popularity increase vote counts, thereby having an effect on revenue of the film?¶

  1. Suprisingly the revenue generated was the highest for the films which had lesser popularity (probably mostly before the release) and movies like Guardians of Galaxy, Interstellar and Mad max fury which had good popularity failed to fair well in the box office.
  2. It is also notable to say that the popularity is inversely linked to average votes which gives a fair indication of the audience acceptance of the movies, resulting in Higher revenues.

4. Does Seasonal releases impact the revenue generation of a movie?¶

  1. Months of May,June and July have resulted in higher revenues for movies being the holiday season for most of the countries and hence the number reflect on the same (Average of 167 Millon USD for May- July and 117 Million USD for August to October ).

  2. Interestingly the Number of releases have come down during these months May,June and July and it has increased between August to October may be because of the local holiday season in the US or in Europe. However the revenue shows a contradictory to this figures as the movies haven’t done well in the box office released during this period (August to October).

5. What factors are associated with the failure of a film?¶

  1. The films which have which haven’t fared well in the box office has been largely associated with the Popularity index with mean of just 0.69 as opposed to 1.71 with the movies generated higher revenue .
  2. Also the budget invested for the films have been on the lower end with 23.3 millions USD as opposed to 66.5 million USD for higher revenue films.
  3. The runtime has also played its part with average runtime of 105 minutes as opposed to 113 minutes for higher revenue films, this may mean that the story plot did not have a complete cycle (a rough guess).

6. What genres are most accepted?¶

Going by the data set, I have summed up on the top 5 genres with Visualization. It turned out Horror, Science Fiction, Adventure, Thriller and Animation are the most accepted genres by the audience with respect to Average revenue and Avg vote count counts.
